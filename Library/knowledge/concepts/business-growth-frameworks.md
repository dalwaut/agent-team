# Business Growth Frameworks

Distilled frameworks for HELM and business management. Source: curated from entrepreneurship content.

## 1-1-1 Rule (Focus Until $1M)

Sell **one product/service** to **one avatar** through **one channel** until you hit $1M revenue. No exceptions.

**Why it works:** Diversifying too early splits attention, dilutes messaging, and prevents mastery of any single acquisition channel. Depth beats breadth at early revenue stages.

**HELM application:**
- When launching a new revenue stream, lock in one offer + one audience + one channel before expanding
- Resist the urge to add products, target new segments, or spread across platforms prematurely
- Expansion signal: consistent demand, delivery dialed in, channel economics proven

## 4-4-4 Operating Cadence

Split working time into three 4-hour blocks:

| Block | Focus | Activities |
|-------|-------|-----------|
| **Promote** (first) | Let people know you exist | Content, outreach, ads, funnel optimization |
| **Deliver** | Fulfill promises to paying customers | Service delivery, support, product work |
| **Build** | Create the future | Opportunity curation, prioritization, systems, learning |

**Key insight:** Promotion goes first because nothing else matters if nobody knows about you. Delivery second because paying customers are the priority. Build last because it's investment in tomorrow.

**HELM application:**
- When planning business operations, ensure all three blocks are covered — most struggling businesses neglect promotion or build
- Audit client businesses against this split to find imbalances

## Top 10% Analysis (Learning Loop)

A structured method for skill acquisition and optimization:

1. **Generate volume** — Do a large number of reps (calls, content pieces, campaigns, etc.)
2. **Identify the top 10%** — Which outputs performed best?
3. **Analyze the difference** — What do the top 10% have in common that the bottom 90% don't?
4. **Isolate key variables** — Not all differences matter equally. Test one variable at a time to find the drivers
5. **Eliminate bottom-90% mistakes, amplify top-10% patterns** — Apply findings to the next batch
6. **Repeat** — Run another cycle. Keep converging toward the top performers

**Why iterations, not hours:** 10,000 iterations beats 10,000 hours because iterations assume failure + feedback + improvement. Hours alone can be flat repetition with no learning.

**HELM application:**
- Apply to content performance analysis (which posts/emails/campaigns outperform?)
- Apply to sales pipeline optimization (which leads convert, what do they have in common?)
- Apply to service delivery (which client outcomes are best, what drove them?)
- This is essentially a manual version of what OPAI agents should do: feedback loops, not guessing

## Maker vs Manager Time Blocking

Two fundamentally different work modes that destroy each other when mixed:

| Mode | Ideal Day | Unit of Work | Enemy |
|------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Maker** | Empty calendar | Deep, uninterrupted blocks (4-6 hrs) | Context switching, notifications, meetings |
| **Manager** | Full calendar | 5-15 min touch points, decisions, coordination | Blank time with nothing to respond to |

**Rule:** Never mix these in the same block. A single Slack message in a maker block costs 20+ minutes of refocus time.

**Practical split:**
- Dedicate specific days or half-days to each mode
- Maker time goes first in the day (mornings) — willpower and focus are highest
- Manager time fills the rest — meetings, calls, messages, coordination

**HELM application:**
- When advising on team productivity or client operations, audit whether makers are being interrupted by manager tasks
- Schedule deep work (strategy, content creation, system building) in protected blocks
- Batch all communications and coordination into dedicated windows

## Spend Framework (Tools / Implementation / Trials)

Three categories for productive business spending:

| Category | What It Means | Examples |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| **Tools** | Software/infrastructure that gives leverage | CRM, landing pages, automation platforms, analytics |
| **Implementation** | Expert help that accelerates execution | Courses, communities, 1:1 tutoring, consulting |
| **Trials** | Money at risk to test and learn | Ad spend, content experiments, MVP launches |

**Key insight:** Saving money is necessary to free up capital, but hoarding it doesn't generate growth. The goal is to redirect spend from lifestyle to these three buckets.

**HELM application:**
- When budgeting for a business, ensure spend maps to one of these three categories
- If a business is stalling, check which category is starved — usually it's trials (not enough experimentation) or implementation (trying to figure everything out alone)
