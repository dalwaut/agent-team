## Candlestick Anatomy

Every candlestick is built from **4 data points**:

| Component | What It Is |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Open** | Price at the start of the time period |
| **High** | Highest price reached during the period |
| **Low** | Lowest price reached during the period |
| **Close** | Price at the end of the time period |

### Structure

```
     ─── High (top of upper wick)
     │
     │   Upper wick (candle wick / tail)
     │
    ┌┤── Close (green) or Open (red)
    ││
    ││   Body (solid filled area)
    ││
    └┤── Open (green) or Close (red)
     │
     │   Lower wick (candle wick / tail)
     │
     ─── Low (bottom of lower wick)
```

- **Green candle**: Open at bottom, Close at top → price went UP
- **Red candle**: Open at top, Close at bottom → price went DOWN
- **Body**: Distance between Open and Close → strength of directional move
- **Wicks/Tails**: Distance beyond the body → rejection / failed moves

### Time Frames

| Time Frame | Users | Candle Duration |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| **1-minute** | Day traders | 60 seconds |
| **5-minute** | Day traders | 5 minutes |
| **Hourly** | Swing traders | 60 minutes |
| **Daily** | Swing traders / investors | Market open → close |

**Key insight**: Candles are LIVE until the period closes. A green candle can flip red with seconds remaining. Watch the clock — candles "print" (become permanent history) only when the period ends.

**Daily candle timing**: Stock market daily candle closes at 4:00 PM EST. Many investors act between 3-4 PM because the daily shape is becoming certain.

## Single Candlestick Patterns

### Bullish → Bearish Spectrum

```
MOST BULLISH                              NEUTRAL                              MOST BEARISH
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Long Body   Dragonfly  Hammer   Short    Doji    Spinning   Inverted   Hanging  Gravestone  Shooting   Long Body
 (Green)     Doji              Body    Standard    Top      Hammer      Man       Doji        Star      (Red)
```

### Long Body Candle

**Shape**: Large solid body, minimal or no wicks
**Signal**: **Strongest sentiment** — decisive directional move

| Color | Meaning | What Happened |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| Green | Strong bullish | Opened at low, surged straight to close at high |
| Red | Strong bearish | Opened at high, dropped straight to close at low |

**Context clues**:
- After sideways action → breakout signal, trend beginning
- Bodies getting progressively BIGGER → momentum building
- Bodies getting progressively SMALLER → trend exhausting

### Short Body Candle

**Shape**: Small solid body, small or no wicks
**Signal**: Mild sentiment — no strong conviction

**Context-dependent interpretation**:
- Short bodies getting LARGER → momentum building (early trend)
- Short bodies getting SMALLER after long bodies → trend exhausting (approaching reversal)
- During sideways consolidation → not significant

### Doji (Candle of Indecision)

**Shape**: Open and Close at same price (cross/plus shape)
**Signal**: **Battle between buyers and sellers — indecision**

| Variation | Shape | Signal | Best Context |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Standard Doji** | Cross with equal upper/lower wicks | Pure indecision | Neutral — context-dependent |
| **Gravestone Doji** | Open/close at bottom, long upper wick | **Bearish** — squeezed up but reversed back down | Top of uptrend → reversal imminent |
| **Dragonfly Doji** | Open/close at top, long lower wick | **Bullish** — dropped but reversed back up | Bottom of downtrend → reversal imminent |

**Why dojis matter at extremes**:
- At the **top** of an uptrend: caution flag → trend about to shift
- At the **bottom** of a downtrend: reversal indicator → buying opportunity
- During **sideways** action: not significant

**Gravestone doji at top**: Price opened, squeezed up, came ALL the way back down → reversal has already begun inside that candle.

**Dragonfly doji at bottom**: Price opened, dropped down, rallied ALL the way back up → reversal has already begun inside that candle.

### Spinning Top

**Shape**: Small body with longer upper AND lower wicks
**Signal**: Indecision — tug-of-war, no clear winner

- Similar to doji but with a small body
- At top/bottom of trends → possible reversal
- During consolidation → not significant
- Slightly green = mild bullish bias; slightly red = mild bearish bias

### Hammer

**Shape**: Small body at TOP, long lower wick, no/minimal upper wick
**Signal**: **Bullish** (at bottom of downtrend)

The name comes from "hammering out a base":
- Price dropped during the period (lower wick)
- Buyers stepped in and pushed price back up to close near the high
- Shows buying support at the low

**Entry approach**:
1. See hammer forming at bottom of selloff
2. Watch clock — 10, 9, 8 seconds left on candle
3. Anticipate next candle will continue higher
4. Entry: as next candle opens and breaks the high
5. Stop-loss: below the hammer's low (the wick bottom)

**Context warning**: A hammer at the TOP of an uptrend → see "Hanging Man" below.

### Hanging Man

**Shape**: Identical to hammer — small body at top, long lower wick
**Signal**: **Bearish** (at top of uptrend)

Same shape as hammer, but opposite context:
- At the top, the drop-and-recovery shows instability
- Sellers are appearing; price struggled to hold
- If closes RED → even worse (stronger bearish signal)
- Named for the ominous implication — price is likely to "fall"

### Inverted Hammer

**Shape**: Small body at BOTTOM, long upper wick, no/minimal lower wick
**Signal**: Weak/ambiguous at bottom of downtrend

- Price squeezed up but sellers pushed it back down
- The topping tail (upper wick) is generally bearish
- Not a preferred buy signal — shows sellers still in control
- Less reliable than a standard hammer at bottoms

### Shooting Star

**Shape**: Identical to inverted hammer — small body at bottom, long upper wick
**Signal**: **Bearish** (at top of uptrend)

Named because "shooting stars come back down to earth":
- Price tried to go higher but got rejected
- Reversal has already begun inside that candle
- More bearish than a hanging man
- Can be green or red — signal is in the shape + context

**Confirmation**: Next candle breaks the low of the shooting star → confirmed reversal.

## Two-Candlestick Patterns

### Tweezer Top (Bearish)

**Shape**: Two candles at top of uptrend, both with upper wicks reaching same level
**Signal**: Bearish reversal — double rejection at resistance

- Like a "double shooting star" — price tried twice and failed
- The matching highs show a ceiling the market can't break
- Strong reversal indicator

### Tweezer Bottom (Bullish)

**Shape**: Two candles at bottom of downtrend, both with lower wicks reaching same level
**Signal**: Bullish reversal — double support hold

- Two hammers back-to-back → strong floor
- Double-bottomed: tried to break lower TWICE and couldn't
- Position of strength — buyers defending aggressively

### Bullish Engulfing

**Shape**: Small red candle followed by large green candle that fully engulfs it (opens below red's low, closes above red's high)
**Signal**: **Very strong bullish reversal**

- Buyers completely overpower all previous selling
- **Primarily a daily chart pattern** — requires a gap down open
- Rarely occurs on intraday charts (1-min, 5-min) because next candle opens at ~same price as previous close

### Bearish Engulfing

**Shape**: Small green candle followed by large red candle that fully engulfs it
**Signal**: **Very strong bearish reversal**

- Sellers completely overpower all previous buying
- Typically caused by overnight news that initially seemed positive, then sentiment reversed
- Also primarily a daily chart pattern

### Morning Star (Bullish)

**Shape**: Three candles — (1) red candle, (2) gap down to hammer/doji, (3) rally candle
**Signal**: Bullish reversal on daily charts

- The middle candle (hammer or doji) gaps below previous close
- Shows selling exhaustion + buyers stepping in
- Confirmation: third candle rallies back up
- **Variation**: Morning Doji Star — middle candle is a doji instead of hammer

**Trading**: Enter between 3-4 PM when daily candle shape is nearly certain.

### Evening Star (Bearish)

**Shape**: Three candles — (1) green candle, (2) gap up to shooting star/doji, (3) sell candle
**Signal**: Bearish reversal on daily charts

- Exact opposite of morning star
- Middle candle gaps above previous close but reverses
- **Variation**: Evening Doji Star — middle candle is a doji

## Three-Candlestick Patterns

### Three White Soldiers (Bullish)

**Shape**: Three consecutive large green candles, each closing higher
**Signal**: Very strong bullish momentum

- Shows sustained buying pressure across 3 periods
- Self-explanatory strength — but don't chase the top of the third candle
- Often followed by a pullback (rising three) before continuation

### Three Black Crows (Bearish)

**Shape**: Three consecutive large red candles, each closing lower
**Signal**: Very strong bearish momentum / selling pressure

- Named from old black & white printing (red = filled black)
- Shows sustained selling across 3 periods
- Often signals major trend reversal or acceleration of downtrend

### Rising Three / Bull Flag (Bullish Continuation)

**Shape**: Strong green candle(s), then 2-3 small pullback candles, then continuation up
**Signal**: Consolidation within uptrend → next leg higher coming

- The pullback candles hold above the base of the initial move
- Classic pattern: 3 white soldiers → rising three (2-3 pullback candles) → 3 more white soldiers
- **Entry**: First candle to break the high after the pullback
- **Stop-loss**: Low of the pullback

### Falling Three / Bear Flag (Bearish Continuation)

**Shape**: Strong red candle(s), then 2-3 small bounce candles, then continuation down
**Signal**: Minor bounce within downtrend → next leg lower coming

- Mirror image of rising three
- Bounce doesn't recover fully → just pause before more selling
- Each sell wave can create new falling three setups

## Confirmation & Entry Rules

### The Reversal Confirmation Rule

A suspected reversal is NOT confirmed until:
- **Bullish reversal**: Next candle breaks the HIGH of the signal candle
- **Bearish reversal**: Next candle breaks the LOW of the signal candle

Until confirmation occurs, the signal is just an *indicator*, not a *guarantee*.

### Entry Timing

| Signal | Entry Point | Stop-Loss |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Hammer at bottom | First candle to go GREEN after hammer | Below hammer's low |
| Shooting star at top | Short on first candle to break star's LOW | Above star's high |
| Dragonfly doji | As soon as next candle turns green (even 3 sec in) | Below doji's low |
| Rising three (bull flag) | First candle to make new HIGH after pullback | Low of pullback |
| Falling three (bear flag) | Short on break of new LOW after bounce | High of bounce |
| Engulfing pattern | Direction of the engulfing candle | Beyond the engulfing candle's extreme |

### Watching Candle Formation in Real-Time

- Track the clock — know when candles are about to close
- A candle's shape changes constantly until it "prints" (period ends)
- Anticipate entries as the signal candle nears completion
- Don't wait for the candle to fully close if the shape is clear with seconds left
- Use Level 2 / time & sales to see order flow inside the current candle

## Rhythmic Pattern Recognition

Markets often establish a **rhythm** within a session:

- If pattern is: 3 up → 2 down → 3 up → 2 down → expect continuation
- If pattern is: 2 up → 3 down → 2 up → 3 down → expect continuation
- The instrument tends to maintain its rhythm once established
- Pay attention to what THIS specific instrument is doing TODAY

## Context Is Everything

### Patterns Mean Different Things in Different Locations

| Pattern | At Top of Move | At Bottom of Move | During Sideways |
|---------|---------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Doji | Bearish warning | Bullish indicator | Not significant |
| Hammer | = Hanging Man (bearish) | Bullish (hammering base) | Not significant |
| Inverted Hammer | = Shooting Star (bearish) | Weak signal | Not significant |
| Spinning Top | Bearish warning | Bullish indicator | Not significant |
| Small bodies | Trend exhausting | Trend exhausting | Normal |

### Common Mistakes

1. **Buying at the top** (capitulation) — seeing green candles and FOMO buying after extended move
2. **Panic selling at the bottom** — seeing red candles and selling after extended drop
3. **Ignoring context** — a hammer means nothing without knowing WHERE in the trend it formed
4. **Not waiting for confirmation** — entering before the next candle confirms the reversal
5. **Trading patterns in sideways action** — dojis and spinning tops during consolidation are meaningless

## Instrument Selection

Candlestick patterns work BEST on:
- **High volatility** instruments moving significantly today
- **High volume** — many traders watching = patterns more reliable
- **Leading percentage gainers** — top movers in the market
- **Catalysts** — news, earnings, FDA approvals, etc.

Applies universally across: stocks, futures, forex, cryptocurrency — the language is the same.
