# AI-Native SaaS Playbook (30 Steps)

**Source**: Greg Isenberg — "Biggest wealth creation opportunity is SaaS"
**Relevance**: v4 "Open Doors" revenue strategy, HELM autonomous business operations

> **Core thesis**: SaaS isn't dead — it's evolving. The new model: sub-niche focus + media-first distribution + agent-powered fulfillment + outcome-based pricing. Lower costs, no VC required, compete against incumbents via bespoke + AI leverage.

---

## Phase 1: Research & Positioning (Steps 1-5)

### 1. Start with a sub-niche inside a big market
- Don't compete in the big market (that's where VC-backed companies play)
- Go bespoke: FIRE within finance, local roofing within construction, etc.
- Target: cash-flowing startup ($100K-$1M/mo), not a unicorn

### 2. Map the sub-niche's workflow end-to-end
- Interview practitioners OR be the practitioner OR use AI to research
- Document every step in the user's daily workflow
- Example (roofing): check leads → respond → qualify → schedule → visit → photos → estimate → quote → follow up → negotiate

### 3. Identify where money changes hands
- These are your wedge points for software
- Examples: negotiate price, collect deposit, order materials, invoice
- Where money moves = where value is highest = where willingness to pay exists

### 4. Spot repetitive mechanical steps
- These are automation/agent opportunities
- Mechanical = no judgment required, just execution
- Example: checking leads across 4 platforms daily = 10 min/day = agent opportunity

### 5. Quantify the cost of those steps
- Hours × hourly rate = dollar value of time saved
- "I'll save you 150 hours/year at $400/hr" = tangible ROI pitch
- This becomes your pricing anchor

---

## Phase 2: Media & Distribution (Steps 6-10)

### 6. Create scroll-stopping content around the workflow
- Pick ONE channel (Instagram, TikTok, X, YouTube)
- Use AI as CMO: research viral patterns in the niche, generate content calendars, script ideas
- Push AI to give non-obvious ideas, not generic content
- Minimum: 1 piece of content per day

### 7. Study posts that get saves, replies, DMs
- Saves = value signal (people want to reference it)
- DMs = buying signal
- Develop intuition for what resonates in your niche

### 8. Double down on organic angles that convert
- Not just engagement — track what leads to signups/revenue

### 9. Run paid ads on proven organic winners
- If it works organically, high chance it works as a paid ad
- Don't run ads on unproven content — let organic validate first

### 10. Capture emails from day one
- Email list = your foundation, your insurance
- When social fails or ads underperform, you can always email your list

---

## Phase 3: Build the Product (Steps 11-18)

### 11. Manually perform the workflow first
- Start as a service business with you at the core
- This builds deep understanding of the actual work
- Not sexy, but essential for product quality

### 12. Document every step precisely
- Every click, every decision, every edge case
- This documentation becomes your agent spec

### 13. Separate judgment from mechanical tasks
- AI excels at mechanical tasks
- AI is inconsistent at judgment calls
- Start by automating mechanical, keep humans on judgment

### 14. Turn mechanical tasks into agent workflows
- This is where the SaaS value lives
- Each mechanical step = potential agent

### 15. Design agents to complete full tasks
- Not partial automation — full task completion
- Agent should handle the entire workflow segment end-to-end

### 16. Connect agents to real tools
- MCP, API integrations: email, Slack, CRM, Stripe, etc.
- Agents need skills and tool access to perform real work
- Start manual, then give agents access incrementally

### 17. Add orchestration, retries, and verifications
- The orchestration layer is the new interface layer
- "Whoever owns the coordination layer owns the flow"
- Build: retries on failure, verification steps, human-in-the-loop checkpoints

### 18. Store user preferences + long-term memory
- User preferences = moat
- Memory makes the product better over time
- Personalization compounds value

---

## Phase 4: Launch & Monetize (Steps 19-23)

### 19. Launch narrow with high-touch onboarding
- Resist the temptation for quick signup flows
- Get deep data during onboarding — it builds your moat
- Balance: enough questions for quality, not so many you lose them

### 20. Publish measurable proof
- Revenue generated, hours saved, costs reduced
- Specific numbers, not vague claims

### 21. Move pricing from per-seat to per-task
- Per-seat model is dying (why big SaaS stocks are down 30-50%)
- Per-task: "Complete this workflow = $X"
- Can start per-seat, but migrate toward per-task

### 22. Shift to outcome pricing
- Ultimate goal: charge for outcomes, not activities
- "We generated X leads" not "We ran the lead gen agent Y times"

### 23. Increase pricing as value compounds
- More workflows + more trust + better results = higher prices
- Value compounds → pricing should follow

---

## Phase 5: Scale & Dominate (Steps 24-30)

### 24. Explore adjacent workflows
- Once your core workflow is dialed, expand to neighboring ones
- Build vs. buy decision: AI makes building so cheap, often better to build

### 25. Orchestrate multiple agents across the lifecycle
- Multiple agents, multiple workflows, one orchestration layer
- The product becomes an execution engine for the niche

### 26. Build switching costs through data and memory
- More data + more memory + more personalization = harder to leave
- This IS your moat (not the code, not the features)

### 27. Turn power users into public case studies
- Don't just write articles — send cameras, film real users
- Put paid spend behind case study content

### 28. Hire operators from inside the niche
- They know the domain, they know the customers
- Reinvest profits into people who can run operations

### 29. Reinvest profits into distribution and product depth
- Go deeper in current workflows AND wider into adjacent ones
- Scale content spend and paid ads

### 30. Become the default execution layer for that sub-niche
- You're not just a tool — you're THE way this niche gets work done
- Default status = ultimate competitive position

## Competitive Advantages of This Model

| Advantage | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **Sub-niche focus** | Compete against VC-backed generalists with bespoke specificity |
| **Media at the core** | Built-in distribution — audience + email + proven ad creative |
| **AI fulfillment** | Lower cost structure than human-powered competitors |
| **Outcome pricing** | Aligns incentives, beats per-seat on value perception |
| **Low overhead** | No VC required, no large team needed initially |
| **Data moat** | User preferences + memory compound over time |
