# Template: Invoice / Payment Reminder (3-Stage)
# Voice: boutabyte-professional
# Trigger: When an invoice is past due — escalates from polite to firm

## Subject Line Options
- Friendly reminder — Invoice #{{invoice_number}}
- Invoice #{{invoice_number}} — Payment reminder
- Past due — Invoice #{{invoice_number}} for {{project_name}}

## Body — First Notice (1-3 days past due)

Hi {{client_name}},

Quick heads up — invoice #{{invoice_number}} for {{amount}} was due on {{due_date}} and it looks like payment hasn't come through yet. These things slip through the cracks sometimes, so just wanted to flag it.

You can pay via {{payment_method}} — the invoice is attached to the original email from {{invoice_send_date}}, or I can resend it if that's easier. Let me know if you have any questions about the charges.

## Body — Second Notice (7-10 days past due)

Hi {{client_name}},

Following up on invoice #{{invoice_number}} for {{amount}}, which was due {{due_date}}. This is now {{days_overdue}} days past the original due date.

If there's an issue with the invoice or if payment is being processed on your end, just let me know so I can update our records. Otherwise, I'd appreciate getting this taken care of at your earliest convenience. {{payment_link_or_instructions}}

## Body — Final Notice (14+ days past due)

Hi {{client_name}},

This is a final reminder regarding invoice #{{invoice_number}} for {{amount}}, originally due {{due_date}}. The balance is now {{days_overdue}} days overdue.

Please arrange payment within the next {{final_deadline}} days. If there are circumstances affecting payment, I'd like to discuss options — feel free to call me directly at {{phone_number}} or reply here.

Per our agreement, {{late_policy}} applies to outstanding balances past {{grace_period}} days.

## Notes
- First notice is warm and assumes honest oversight — no pressure
- Second notice is direct but professional — states the facts clearly
- Final notice introduces consequences without being aggressive
- Always offer a way to discuss if they're having payment issues
- Check with Dallas before sending Final Notice — some clients get more flexibility
- Never threaten legal action in email — that escalation happens offline
- Include payment link when available to reduce friction
