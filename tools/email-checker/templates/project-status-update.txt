# Template: Weekly Project Status Update
# Voice: client-update
# Trigger: Weekly status email to client — typically sent Monday or Friday

## Subject Line Options
- {{project_name}} — Weekly Update ({{date_range}})
- Status Update: {{project_name}} — Week of {{week_start_date}}
- {{project_name}} Progress Update — {{current_date}}

## Body

Hi {{client_name}},

Here's your weekly update on {{project_name}}.

This past week we wrapped up {{completed_item_1}}, {{completed_item_2}}, and {{completed_item_3}}. {{brief_context_on_most_significant_item}} — this was an important step because {{why_it_matters_to_client}}.

Coming up next, our focus shifts to {{upcoming_item_1}} and {{upcoming_item_2}}. We expect to have {{next_milestone}} ready by {{next_milestone_date}}. {{optional_preview_or_link}}

{{blocker_paragraph_if_applicable}}

Overall we're {{on_track_status}} with the original timeline. {{next_check_in_sentence}} If anything comes up or you have questions in the meantime, just reply here.

## Blocker Paragraph (use only if blockers exist)

One thing I want to flag — we're waiting on {{blocker_description}} before we can move forward with {{blocked_item}}. If you could {{client_action_needed}} by {{needed_by_date}}, that would keep us on schedule. No stress if you need more time, just wanted to keep you in the loop.

## Notes
- Use the "client-update" voice profile for proper tone
- Lead with accomplishments — clients want to see progress
- Be specific about deliverables, not vague ("finished the homepage design" not "made good progress")
- Only include the blocker paragraph if there are actual blockers — do not fabricate urgency
- on_track_status should be one of: "on track", "slightly ahead of schedule", "about a day behind on X but catching up"
- Include a link to staging/preview when available — clients love seeing their project live
- Keep to 3-4 paragraphs max — detailed breakdowns go in project management tools, not email
